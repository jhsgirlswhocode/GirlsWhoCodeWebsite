Current Events

Bejing 2016: One year ago, Li Tingting and a group of feminists were detained by authorities in China on International Womens' Day. Li and four other women were detained on March 7, 2015, for planning to hand out leaflets the following day warning about groping on public transit. The incident caused a public uproar. The hashtag #freethefive trended on social media. Li Tingting stands strong, sharing a photo of herself dressed as Rosie the Riveter one year after the incident. However, the charges against the women still stand today. The womens' lawyers issued a joint statement adressed to the Supreme People's Procuratorate and the National People's Congress. "From then until now, not a single legal body of state has answered our questions." Said a lawyer for one of the women. On March 1, 2016, China's first law against domestic violence took effect. Li looks at the new law as a sign of progress. "Like all social movements, the Chinese feminist movement has experienced climaxes and bottoms," wrote Li. "Although it is greatly restrained at the present, we believe that the feminism activists in China will promote it with our wisdome and brave heart."

1/27/2016: Tawian Elects First Woman President Leader of Taiwan's Democratic Progressive Party (DPP), Tsai Ing-wen, a scholar with degrees from Cornell University and the London School of Economics, won the presidential election, becoming the first female president Taiwan's history. In her victory speech, Tsai claimed the "new era" in Taiwan was only beginning.She promised to resolve major political issues by working with other political parties and involving the public's input.
